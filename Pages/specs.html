GLOSSARY

RESISTANCE: This is one of the most critical parameters of speed control operation. Low on-resistance allows maximum power to be delivered from the batteries, through the speed control, and to the motor. Tekin's two top speed controls, the G-9 and the G-12cIII are the lowest on-resistance speed controls in the world.

DRIVE CURRENT: This is the rated current handling of the MOSFET transistors used in the speed control. The actual peak current that the batteries can deliver is actually only about 120 amps, but a high transistor rating indicates better grade MOSFETS.

B.E.C The BEC is a circuit that eliminates the need to use a seperate receiver battery to power the receiver and servo. A high amp BEC is important to power high speed servos. Tekin uses 5 amp BEC's in our competition units.

INPUT POWER: This is the maximum number of cells you may connect to the speed control.

POWER WIRES: This is the gauge of the power wire used. The smaller the number, the better, as more current will flow.
